Call for Student Research Forum (SRF)


Call for SRF PDF: Download PDF

The IEEE International Symposium on Smart Electronic Systems (IEEE iSES 2025) invites proposals for the Student Research Forum (SRF). This forum offers an opportunity for recently graduated or currently enrolled third/final year B.Tech/M.Tech/MCA/Ph.D. students to present their thesis or project work to a wide audience from academia and industry.

The SRF is a unique platform to showcase innovative work in the following domains:
  • Nanoelectronic, VLSI and Sensor Systems (NVS)
  • Energy-Efficient, Reliable VLSI Systems (ERS)
  • Hardware/Software for the Internet of smart Things and Healthcare (IoTH)
  • Hardware for Secure Information Processing (SIP)
  • Hardware/Software Solutions for AI & Big Data (SAB)
  • Cyber Physical Systems and Social Networks (CSN)
Industry and academic professionals will get a glimpse into emerging innovations from the next generation of researchers in Smart Electronics.

Eligibility:
  • Open to Ph.D. students or final/third-year B.Tech/M.Tech/MCA students currently engaged in research.
  • Research topics must be relevant to the Smart Electronics domain.
  • Repeat SRF presenters must ensure at least 70% new technical content compared to their previous submission.

Submission Guidelines:
Authors must submit a short paper (2–4 pages) in IEEE double-column conference format that includes:
  • Title, Authors, and Affiliations
  • Abstract
  • Introduction and Motivation
  • Proposed Design
  • Conclusions and References
  • The first author must be a student.
  • Submit a single PDF through the Microsoft CMT system.
  • All submissions will undergo peer review.
  • Accepted papers will be published in the official IEEE conference proceedings (IEEE Xplore).

Benefits to Selected Researchers:
  • 4-page publication in IEEE iSES 2025 proceedings (IEEE Xplore indexed)
  • Presentation in the SRF session
  • Networking opportunities with industry and academic leaders
  • Travel grants for student first authors of accepted submissions
  • Encouragement for submissions that demonstrate innovation and strong use cases

For SRF-related queries, please contact:

  • Dr. Geetanjali Sharma – gsharma@msit.in
