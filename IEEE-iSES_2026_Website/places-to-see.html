Places to See

Temple Town Ponda (Shri Mangesh & Shanta Durga)

Ponda is known for its temple circuit—popular stops include Shri Mangesh Temple (Priol) and Shanta Durga Temple (Kavlem), along with other heritage temples in the region.

Image via Wikimedia Commons (view author & license on file page)

Big Foot Goa (Ancestral Goa), Loutolim

A museum and theme park in Loutolim (South Goa) that showcases rural Goan life and culture through life-size installations and traditional settings.

Image via Wikimedia Commons (view author & license on file page)

Basilica of Bom Jesus (Old Goa)

A significant historical and architectural landmark in Old Goa, widely visited for its heritage value and iconic church façade.

Image via Wikimedia Commons (view author & license on file page)

Fort Aguada

A coastal fort offering panoramic views of the Arabian Sea and the surrounding coastline—great for photos and sunset views.

Image via Wikimedia Commons (view author & license on file page)

Fontainhas (Latin Quarter, Panaji)

The historic Latin Quarter of Panaji—famous for old Portuguese-era architecture, colorful lanes, and a calm walking experience.

Image via Wikimedia Commons (view author & license on file page)

Chapora Fort (Near Vagator)

A historical fort overlooking the Chapora River, close to Vagator Beach—known for breezy viewpoints and wide river/sea vistas.

Image via Wikimedia Commons (view author & license on file page)

Dudhsagar Falls

A majestic multi-tiered waterfall set amidst lush greenery—one of Goa’s most famous natural attractions (seasonal flow varies).

Image via Wikimedia Commons (view author & license on file page)

Salim Ali Bird Sanctuary (Chorão Island)

A haven for bird watchers located on Chorão Island—best enjoyed via quiet trails and boat-backed mangrove views.

Image via Wikimedia Commons (view author & license on file page)

Divar Island

A scenic island experience with a slower pace of life—good for peaceful exploration, local views, and a different side of Goa.

Image via Wikimedia Commons (view author & license on file page)

Spice Plantations (Goa Hinterland)

Explore the fragrant hinterland with tours of spice farms—an easy way to experience Goa beyond the beaches.

Image via Wikimedia Commons (view author & license on file page)

Baga & Calangute (North Goa)

Known for water sports, shacks, and a lively atmosphere—often busy and crowded, especially in peak season.

Image via Wikimedia Commons (view author & license on file page)

Palolem / Agonda / Benaulim (South Goa)

Scenic and peaceful beaches—ideal for calm, family-friendly leisure time and a less crowded vibe.

Image via Wikimedia Commons (view author & license on file page)

Vagator (North Goa)

Offers a more laid-back, scenic vibe and is a convenient pairing with nearby Chapora Fort viewpoints.

Image via Wikimedia Commons (view author & license on file page)

Anjuna Flea Market (North Goa)

Famous for its flea market and vibrant vibe—great for souvenir shopping and browsing local crafts.

Image via Wikimedia Commons (view author & license on file page)

Mapusa Friday Market

A popular local market experience for shopping local goods and souvenirs—best known for its busy Friday market energy.

Image via Wikimedia Commons (view author & license on file page)

Mandovi River Cruise (Sunset Cruises)

Sunset cruises on the Mandovi River are a popular evening experience—nice for relaxed views and a break after busy days.

Image via Wikimedia Commons (view author & license on file page)

Naval Aviation Museum (Bogmalo)

A museum visit option in Bogmalo—good for an easy indoor plan and a unique aviation-themed experience.

Image via Wikimedia Commons (view author & license on file page)
