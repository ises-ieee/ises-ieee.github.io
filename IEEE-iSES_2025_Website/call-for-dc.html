Design Contest: High-Speed Hardware Accelerators for Cryptographic Functions


Call for Design Contest PDF: Download PDF


Secure communication protects data privacy and prevents unauthorized access, theft, and modification using encryption, authentication, and integrity checks. This design contest focuses on high-speed, power/area-efficient hardware implementations for widely used cryptographic functions suitable for FPGA or ASIC integration.

Task

Design and implement a hardware accelerator optimized for high throughput, low latency, and energy efficiency for one of:

  • AES-128
  • RSA-2048
  • HMAC (MD5 or SHA-1)

Requirements

  • Supported Algorithm: AES-128, RSA-2048, or HMAC (MD5/SHA-1)
  • High-Speed Architecture: propose/implement optimizations (e.g., pipelining, parallelism, resource sharing, fast adders/multipliers)
  • Performance: support high data rates and low latency
  • Power/Area: analyze and optimize power and silicon area
  • Verification: show functional correctness with standard vectors; report throughput, latency, and (where possible) power
  • Deliverables: RTL (Verilog/VHDL/SystemVerilog); target platform (FPGA—Xilinx/Intel—or ASIC flow); technical report (architecture, optimizations, results, trade-offs); live demo/simulation/FPGA prototype

Evaluation Criteria

  • Correctness (standard vectors)
  • Throughput, Latency, Max Frequency
  • Power and Area Efficiency
  • Design Innovation
  • Documentation & Presentation Quality

Important Dates (2025)

  • Launch: 14 October 2025
  • Last date to receive application: 30 October 2025
  • Orientation session: 3 November 2025
  • Last day of implemented idea submission: 30 November 2025

Submission

Please submit via Microsoft CMT under Design Contest – HW accelerators for Cryptographic functions: https://cmt3.research.microsoft.com/User/Login?ReturnUrl=%2FIEEEiSeS2025
